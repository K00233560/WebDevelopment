Choosing Fiddle Size

Before you start to learn how to play the fiddle, you must decide on a fiddle size that suits you. You must be able to comfortably move your hands and fingers along the entire neck of the fiddle, and your left arm must be mobile to fully maneuver between all four strings. If you are unable to bend your left arm at the elbow and hold the instrument properly, you will be unable to play the notes correctly, and it will be difficult to play in tune. Also, playing a fiddle that is too big will make your arms tired and sore, and may also hurt your neck and back eventually.

Please see Fiddle Size Guide below to help you choose a suitably sized fiddle

FIDDLE SIZE GUIDE
Fiddle Size 1/16 1/10 1/8 1/4 1/2 3/4 Full Size
Suitable Arm Length 33.5 cm or less 36cm 38.5cm 44cm 48.5cm 52cm 54cm or longer

Arm Length = Length from side of neck to wrist
(all sizes are approximations)

Fiddle Parts

  • Body:
    the hourglass shaped hollow body provides amplification for the strings
  • Pegs:
    for tuning the strings – the pegs are twisted up or down to tighten or loosen the string depending on the tone you want
  • Scroll:
    has no specific function – it is mainly for decoration
  • f-holes:
    where the sound comes out of the body
  • Bridge:
    transmits the vibration of those strings to the body
  • Tailpiece:
    connects the strings from the pegs to the other end of the fiddle
  • Chinrest:
    to help the player support the fiddle with their chin while playing
  • Strings:
    the fiddle has 4 strings tuned to the notes g, d, a and e.
