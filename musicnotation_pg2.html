Music Notation Basics

Before learning about the fiddle, let's briefly look at the basics of music notation. (All music notation within this website is in the KEY of D major.)


ABC Notation

ABC notation is a shorthand form of musical notation. In basic form it uses the letters A through G to represent the given notes. This is a simple diagram demonstrating how the ABC notation matches up to the Solfege Scale.

You can follow the diagram while listening to the audio (right of diagram) to become familiar with how each note sounds in the scale.

Sheet Music Notation

Sheet music is a handwritten or printed form of music notation that uses musical symbols to indicate the pitches (melodies), rhythms or chords of a song or instrumental musical piece.

Sheet music is usually noted using the Staff (and tablature.) The staff consists of five horizontal lines on which musical notes lie. The lines and spaces between the lines represent different pitches - lower pitches are lower on the staff, and higher pitches are higher on the staff.

Lines:


Working from the bottom of the staff up, the notes are:

  1. Bottom line = E
  2.       2nd line= G
  3.       3rd line = B
  4.       4th line = D
  5.       5th line = F

An easy acronym to remember the notes on the lines is "Every good boy deserves Fanta".

Spaces:

Working from the bottom of the staff up, the notes are:

  1. (Below bottom line = D)
  2.               1st space = F
  3.             2nd space = A
  4.              3rd space = C
  5.              4rd space = E

The notes between the spaces are easier to remember as they spell out the word "FACE"
